# Project Management Playbook
### Building Delivery Infrastructure from the Ground Up

When I joined this project, there were no documented processes — no Agile ceremonies, no change management workflow, no governance standards, no release framework. Three months in, I was promoted to Project Manager. Over the next year I scaled the team from 3 to 10, delivered multiple platform phases on time, and built the entire operational infrastructure from scratch.

This repository is that infrastructure.

These aren't theoretical templates. Every document here was written, tested against real delivery challenges, revised based on team and client feedback, and actively used in a production environment — a cloud-based data platform (Power BI + Azure Data Factory + SQL) serving 900+ institutions. They reflect what it actually takes to run a cross-functional technical team end-to-end: not just writing a process document, but getting engineers, designers, analysts, and senior stakeholders to align around it and use it consistently.

---

## Why This Is on GitHub

Most PMs keep their process artifacts in a shared drive somewhere. I'm putting mine here because:

- **Transparency over claims.** It's easy to write "established Agile processes" on a résumé. These documents show exactly what that looked like in practice — the decisions made, the tradeoffs acknowledged, the edge cases handled.
- **These are cross-functional artifacts.** The AI governance policy influenced how the engineering team adopted new tools. The ADA guidelines were embedded directly into development standards. The bug approval SOP came out of a real breakdown in how engineering and client teams were communicating. This isn't PM paperwork — it's organizational design.
- **Process is a product.** I believe how a team works is as important as what they build. These documents are the product of that belief.
